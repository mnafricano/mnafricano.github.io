A Note Before We Begin Imagine a library on fire. Not the building ,  the knowledge. Imagine that every book ever written was going to vanish at midnight, and you had until then to save what mattered most. Not sentiment, not sentimental favorites, not the ones you were supposed to have read in college. What would you carry out? That thought experiment produced this book. What follows is not a summary. Summaries are like photographs of meals ,  they tell you what was served but give you nothing to eat. Instead, this is a genuine attempt to synthesize: to find the deepest patterns running through human knowledge, strip away the scaffolding that individual books require for their particular arguments, and present what remains ,  the raw, essential understanding that a person needs to navigate reality. Think of it this way: if the accumulated wisdom of humanity were a river, most books are small tributaries. This book tries to trace the river itself ,  to find where all those streams are actually going, and to hand you a map of the whole watershed at once. There is an order to things. A person who doesn't understand their own mind cannot understand science. A person without science cannot understand history. A person without history cannot understand philosophy. A person without philosophy cannot understand society. And a person without all of these is poorly equipped to face what's coming next, or to build a life worth living. So we begin at the beginning. Not with facts, but with something more foundational: the tool you use to evaluate all facts. We begin with your mind.
